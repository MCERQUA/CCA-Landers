Frequently Asked Questions About Frozen Goods Insurance

What types of insurance do frozen goods businesses need?

Frozen yogurt shops and ice cream businesses typically need: - Product Liability Insurance - Spoilage Coverage - Equipment Breakdown Insurance - General Liability Insurance - Workers' Compensation Insurance - Commercial Property Insurance

Each covers different risks specific to temperature-sensitive food businesses.

How much does insurance cost for a frozen yogurt shop?

Insurance costs vary based on: - Annual revenue - Number of employees - Location and climate risks - Equipment value - Coverage limits

Most small frozen goods businesses pay $1,500-$4,000 annually for comprehensive coverage.

What does spoilage coverage protect against?

Spoilage coverage protects your business from losses due to:

  • Power outages - Equipment failures - Refrigeration system breakdowns - Natural disasters

It covers the cost of spoiled inventory and lost profits during downtime.

Do I need special coverage for my soft serve machines?

Yes, soft serve machines and other specialized equipment should have:

  • Equipment breakdown coverage - Business interruption protection - Contamination coverage

These protect against costly repairs and lost revenue if equipment fails.

Why is product liability important for frozen food businesses?

Product liability is critical because:

  1. Foodborne illness claims can be expensive 2. Allergen contamination risks exist 3. Temperature control failures can cause sickness 4. Product recalls may be necessary 5. Customer lawsuits can damage your reputation

Proper coverage protects your business from these unique risks.
